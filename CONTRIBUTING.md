# Contributing to SmartSepsis-Oph

Thank you for your interest. SmartSepsis-Oph is in Phase 0 (computational
design) and we are actively seeking collaborators, advisors, and partners.
Below is the honest map of how to engage.

## What we are looking for

### 1. Wet-lab partners (highest priority)

We need a laboratory with capability in:
- CRISPR-Dx (Cas12a / Cas13 collateral cleavage assays)
- Isothermal amplification (RPA, LAMP)
- Bacterial culture and DNA extraction at standard biosafety levels
- Optional: paper-strip / lateral flow prototyping

In exchange we offer:
- Joint design of the experimental plan, starting with *S. aureus* / mecA
- Co-authorship on the framework preprint and on subsequent papers
- Open data sharing and reproducible analysis
- Visibility in the project's public communication

### 2. Senior scientific advisors

We are seeking advisors in:
- CRISPR-based diagnostics (SHERLOCK, DETECTR, and successors)
- Ocular microbiology and ophthalmic infectious disease
- Molecular pathology and clinical diagnostic regulation
- Isothermal amplification chemistry

Advisor role can be light-touch (named advisor + occasional review) or
deeper. We are honest about the program's stage: we will not ask you to
endorse claims we have not yet substantiated.

### 3. Clinical partners

Hospitals or eye centers with case volume in:
- Post-cataract endophthalmitis
- Post-injection (anti-VEGF) endophthalmitis
- Microbial keratitis (especially contact lens–related)
- Neonatal ophthalmia

For future prospective sampling under IRB.

### 4. Funders

Seed-stage funders interested in AI-first diagnostics for neglected sample
types. We are open to grants, philanthropic capital, and aligned strategic
investment.

## How to start a conversation

1. **Email:** iaparamedicos@gmail.com with a brief note about your
   interest and what you can bring.
2. **Issue:** open an issue with the `partnership`, `advisor`, or `funding`
   label.
3. **Direct:** reach out to João Victor Pacheco Dias on LinkedIn.

## Code contributions

The codebase is in active research-prototype state. We welcome:
- Bug fixes and reproducibility improvements
- Documentation and tutorial contributions
- Independent validation of the design pipeline outputs
- Additions to the guide-RNA design and scoring modules

Please open an issue before submitting a substantial PR so we can align on
scope.

## What we ask of contributors

- **Honesty about scope.** Do not introduce code or documentation that
  overstates the program's stage or makes performance claims that have not
  been experimentally validated.
- **Reproducibility.** Provide enough information for someone else to run
  what you ran.
- **Attribution.** When borrowing methods from published work, cite
  properly.

## Code of conduct

Be excellent to each other. We will publish a formal Code of Conduct ahead
of the framework preprint. Until then, the spirit of the Contributor
Covenant applies.
